Why should we use Disciplined Agile Delivery?

Flexibility: It allows teams to tailor their approach based on the context of the project.
Scalability: DAD supports scaling agile practices across large and complex organizations.
End-to-End Focus:Unlike traditional agile frameworks that focus mainly on construction, DAD covers the entire delivery lifecycle.
Risk Management: It emphasizes early risk identification and mitigation, enhancing the chances of project success.

What is Disciplined Agile Delivery?

Disciplined Agile Delivery is a hybrid agile framework that combines practices and principles from various agile methods such as Scrum, Kanban, Extreme Programming (XP), and Lean.

How do we implement Disciplined Agile Delivery?

Inception Phase

Start with a clear understanding of the project goals, scope, and constraints. Plan the architecture and initial backlog.

Construction Phase

Develop the product incrementally, focusing on delivering potentially shippable increments.

Transition Phase

Ensure the product is ready for deployment. This includes final testing, documentation, and deployment planning.

What If Scenarios in DAD?

What if requirements change frequently? DADâs iterative approach allows for frequent reassessment and incorporation of changing requirements. Regular stakeholder feedback sessions and adaptive planning help manage changes effectively.
What if the team is geographically distributed? DAD provides guidance on practices for distributed teams, such as using collaborative tools, conducting virtual meetings, and maintaining clear communication channels to ensure alignment and coordination.
What if there are tight deadlines? Prioritize high-value features and focus on delivering minimum viable products (MVPs) incrementally. Use techniques like timeboxing and effective scope management to ensure timely delivery.
What if there are compliance and regulatory requirements? DAD incorporates strategies for dealing with governance, risk, and compliance (GRC) throughout the project lifecycle, ensuring that regulatory requirements are met without compromising agility.
